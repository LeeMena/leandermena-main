90-Day Framework

Pre-Opening Phases

A battle-tested timeline for taking a restaurant concept from signed lease to opening night, without the chaos.

Days 1–30

Foundation

  • Concept & brand clarification
  • Financial modeling & budget build
  • Permits, licensing & compliance roadmap
  • Vendor sourcing & contract negotiation
  • Menu R&D kick-off
Days 31–60

Build-Out

  • Hiring pipeline & interview frameworks
  • SOP development (FOH & BOH)
  • POS & tech stack configuration
  • Supply chain finalization
  • Training curriculum design
Days 61–80

Training

  • Full staff onboarding & training
  • Service standards rehearsal
  • Health & safety certification
  • Menu tastings & costing sign-off
  • Dry-run service sessions
Days 81–90

Launch

  • Soft opening execution
  • Guest feedback loops
  • Real-time ops adjustments
  • Grand opening planning
  • Post-launch performance review

Chapter 1

Concept & Brand Clarity

Before a single dollar is spent on build-out, your concept must be defined with surgical precision. Who is your guest? What is the one thing you do better than anyone?

  • Define your guest avatar in writing
  • Lock your cuisine, price point & service style
  • Establish brand voice & visual identity
  • Validate concept against local competitive set
  • Align ownership team on non-negotiables
Leander Mena

F&B Operations Consultant & Pre-Opening Consultant, Miami

Chapter 2

Financial Structure & Modeling

A restaurant that opens without a validated financial model is a restaurant that closes within 18 months. Build the model before you sign anything.

  • Pro forma P&L with 3 scenarios
  • Prime cost targets by daypart
  • Break-even cover count analysis
  • CapEx budget with 15% contingency
  • Cash flow projection for months 1–6
