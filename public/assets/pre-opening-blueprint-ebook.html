Introduction

About this blueprint

Start at Day -120

This framework was built over 18 years and 15+ restaurant openings across Miami, from Michelin-chef concepts to luxury hotel F&B. It was the backbone of operations at Maska Indian Kitchen + Bar, which opened on schedule, achieved a 4.7+ opening week, and delivered 26% labor by week three.

It also reflects banquet revenue recovery work at SLS Brickell, where revenue improved 31% in 90 days. Every item in this checklist has been tested, refined, and stress-tested under real opening-night pressure.

"If you don't get the first 30 days right, every later fix becomes more expensive."
Operating principle

How to use this blueprint

  1. Start at Day -120 or wherever the project currently sits.
  2. Assign an owner to every deliverable.
  3. Use the weekly scorecard to track progress and weak spots.
  4. Flag risks early; a fix at Day -30 costs far more than the same fix at Day -90.

Phase 1

Foundation

Days -120 to -90

Concept before spend
"If you don't get this right, everything else is expensive band-aids."
Week 1–2

Concept clarification

  • Finalize concept statement in 25 words or less
  • Define target guest persona: demographics, psychographics, occasion
  • Lock menu direction with chef or culinary lead
  • Confirm price point strategy: check average and high-low range
  • Establish brand voice and service style: casual, polished, experiential
  • Create mood board for interior, plating, and uniform direction
  • Sign off on floor plan with kitchen flow analysis
Week 3–4

Financial framework

  • Build 24-month P&L projection: conservative, base, optimistic
  • Set opening budget cap and contingency, with 15% buffer preferred
  • Define labor cost target by month 3, 6, and 12
  • Establish food cost target and vendor negotiation strategy
  • Create pre-opening cash flow model through break-even
  • Secure funding or investor sign-off on budget
  • Set up accounting system and chart of accounts
Week 5–6

Legal & permits

  • File for business entity: LLC, Corp, etc.
  • Apply for EIN
  • Submit liquor license application immediately
  • Submit food service license application
  • Schedule health department pre-inspection walkthrough
  • Confirm zoning compliance for seating, signage, and hours
  • Review lease terms for TI allowance, kick-out clauses, and key money
  • Secure general liability, property, and workers' comp insurance

Week 7–8

Team architecture

  • Build organizational chart with all positions and reporting lines
  • Define salary bands and total compensation for every role
  • Draft offer letters and employment agreements
  • Create hiring timeline showing when each role must be filled
  • Identify key hire risks for hard-to-fill roles in the local market
  • Set up payroll system: Gusto, ADP, or equivalent
  • Design benefits package where applicable
Phase 1 checkpoint

Before moving to buildout

The concept, budget, licenses, org chart, and opening labor targets should already be clear before procurement starts. This is where operator confidence is earned: every later decision gets easier when the business model is already disciplined.

"Clarity compounds. Chaos compounds faster."
Pre-opening operating truth
04
Phase 2

Buildout & procurement

Days -90 to -60

Margin decisions happen here
"The decisions made here determine your operating margins for the next three years."
Week 9–10

Vendor matrix & negotiation

  • Issue RFPs to at least three vendors per category
  • Protein: beef, poultry, seafood
  • Produce
  • Dry goods and pantry
  • Beverage: alcohol and non-alcohol
  • Linens and uniforms
  • Smallwares and equipment
  • Cleaning and chemicals
  • Score vendors on price, reliability, quality, delivery, terms, backup, certifications, and relationship
  • Negotiate payment terms: Net 30 minimum, Net 45 ideal
  • Lock order quantities and delivery schedule
  • Establish a backup vendor for every critical category
  • Set up vendor file with contacts, account numbers, and cutoff times

Week 11–12

Equipment & smallwares

  • Finalize kitchen equipment list with chef
  • Order long-lead items: custom hoods, walk-ins, specialty equipment
  • Purchase smallwares: pots, pans, utensils, thermometers
  • Order POS system and test integration
  • Set up reservation system: Resy, OpenTable, Tock
  • Purchase uniforms and approve samples
  • Order menu boards, signage, and wayfinding
Week 13–14

Menu engineering

  • Finalize recipes with exact specs: yield, portion, plating
  • Cost every menu item to the penny
  • Run theoretical food cost analysis with 28–32% target for full-service
  • Design menu layout with eye-tracking principles
  • Price using competitor analysis plus cost and perceived value
  • Identify high-margin stars and low-margin dogs
  • Create modifier and upsell strategy
  • Build allergen matrix and dietary accommodation protocol
Week 15–16

Tech stack

  • Configure POS with full menu, modifiers, and pricing
  • Set up inventory management system
  • Configure reservation platform with table map
  • Set up online ordering if applicable
  • Configure payroll and scheduling software
  • Set up CRM or guest database
  • Create email marketing account: Klaviyo or Mailchimp
  • Set up Google Business Profile and social accounts
06
Phase 3

People & process

Days -60 to -30

Team before launch
"You can fix a broken plate. You can't fix a broken team on opening night."
Week 17–18

Hiring sprint

  • Post all remaining positions
  • Conduct interviews with a structured scorecard, not gut feel
  • Check references for every management hire
  • Extend offers with clear start dates
  • Schedule pre-opening training calendar
  • Order training materials and handbooks
  • Plan a "meet the team" event for culture-building
Week 19–20

SOP development

  • Write opening procedure: who unlocks, what time, checklist
  • Write closing procedure: cash out, cleaning, security
  • Write server sequence of service
  • Write line cook prep standards
  • Write inventory management SOP
  • Write guest recovery protocol
  • Write cash handling and tip pooling policy
  • Write health and safety compliance checklist

Week 21–22

Training program

  • Build a two-week training curriculum
  • Day 1–2: Culture, mission, tour, introductions
  • Day 3–4: Menu knowledge; taste every dish, know every ingredient
  • Day 5–6: Service standards and role-specific skills
  • Day 7–8: POS training and mock orders
  • Day 9–10: Shadow shifts and feedback loops
  • Day 11–12: Team service practice and mock service
  • Day 13–14: Soft opening prep and final assessments
  • Create training manual: digital and printed
  • Schedule trainer assignments and plan daily huddle talking points
Week 23–24

Soft opening plan

  • Set soft opening dates: 3–5 nights, limited covers recommended
  • Create invite list: friends, family, industry, media
  • Design feedback collection system: digital survey + manager table touches
  • Plan menu for soft opening: full menu or limited version
  • Set comp budget for soft opening
  • Schedule post-soft-opening debrief meetings
  • Create social media content calendar for opening buzz
08
Phase 4

Final sprint

Days -30 to opening

Eliminate, don't add
"The last 30 days are about elimination, not addition."
Week 25–26

Pre-launch checks

  • Final health department inspection walkthrough
  • Fire department inspection and certificate of occupancy
  • Liquor license final approval and posted
  • All permits displayed visibly
  • POS fully programmed and tested with dummy transactions
  • Music licensing secured: ASCAP, BMI, SESAC
  • Final equipment calibration: ovens, fryers, refrigeration temps
  • Emergency contact list posted: plumber, electrician, HVAC, exterminator
Week 27–28

Staff readiness

  • All staff hired and onboarded
  • Uniforms distributed and fitted
  • Schedule published through opening week
  • All staff pass menu knowledge test: 80% minimum
  • Mock service completed with full team
  • Identify weak performers and assign extra coaching
  • Confirm opening-night staffing levels with 20% overstaffing

Week 29–30

Opening week execution

  • Day -7: Final deep clean of the entire space
  • Day -6: Inventory delivery and storage organization
  • Day -5: Staff walkthrough of every station
  • Day -4: Friends & Family night #1 at 50% capacity
  • Day -3: Friends & Family night #2 at 75% capacity
  • Day -2: Final team meeting, role assignments, pep talk
  • Day -1: Rest. No work. Leader needs to be sharp.
  • Day 0: Opening night
Opening week playbook

Daily rhythm

Pre-service
  • Manager walkthrough: lighting, music, temperature, cleanliness
  • Line check: every station, ingredient, and temp
  • Pre-shift huddle: 10 minutes, 3 priorities, 1 focus
  • POS test: one test transaction per terminal
  • Reservation review: VIPs, occasions, dietary notes
During service
  • Manager floor presence, not office presence
  • 2-minute table touches on every table
  • Kitchen timing checks every 15 minutes
  • Real-time feedback collection: verbal, not formal
  • Social media content capture with guest permission
Post-service
  • Team debrief: what worked, what didn't, one fix for tomorrow
  • Revenue reconciliation and cash deposit
  • Prep list for tomorrow
  • Manager log entry
  • Thank-you message to VIPs or media

Red flags: when to pause and fix

  1. Health inspection failure: do not open. Period.
  2. Less than 70% of kitchen team hired by Day -30: push opening date.
  3. Food cost theoretical above 35%: re-engineer menu before printing.
  4. No backup vendor for protein: single-source protein is a shutdown risk.
  5. POS not tested with real transactions: opening night is not a test environment.
  6. Chef hasn't tasted final menu with full team: menu knowledge gap becomes service failure.
  7. No guest recovery protocol: one bad opening-night experience can live forever online.
