The Miami Pre-Opening Playbook

By Leander Mena Miami F&B Operations 8 min read

Opening a restaurant in Miami requires more than a great concept and a lease. The 90 days before doors open determine whether a team performs under pressure or collapses under it.

Days 90–60: Foundation

The first phase is about building the operating backbone before a single hire is made. That means walking the space, understanding the concept deeply, mapping the service flow, and writing the first draft of every core SOP. Most operators skip this phase because it doesn't feel productive — there's nothing to photograph, no one to introduce. But the decisions made here determine whether the system works six months after opening.

  • Space walkthrough and operational gap assessment
  • Vendor identification and preliminary outreach
  • Org chart and staffing plan by role and phase
  • Draft service flow for each daypart
  • Opening budget alignment with ownership

“The budget conversation is the one most operators avoid. Have it on paper, with a shared model — not at week four when you’re already over-committed.”

Days 60–30: Hiring and Systems

Miami's hospitality labor market is competitive. If you start recruiting your management team at the 30-day mark, you'll be settling. Hiring has to begin at 60 days out, and structured onboarding has to start the first day a new manager walks in — not after you finish everything else.

  • Management team hired and contributing to SOP development
  • Line staff recruitment — front-of-house before back-of-house
  • POS configured, not just installed — reporting structure, comp flows, modifier logic
  • Menu training materials and knowledge tests drafted
  • Vendor contracts finalized with confirmed delivery schedules

Days 30–0: Training and Execution

The final 30 days are the most intense stretch of any pre-opening project. Soft opens, staff meals, mock services, and final walkthroughs compress weeks of learning into days. The goal isn't a perfect soft open — it's a team that has made its mistakes in front of forgiving guests, not a full house on opening night.

  • Two to three soft opening services with real guests at reduced capacity
  • Daily pre-shift meetings and written post-service debriefs
  • Final SOP sign-off by every manager before first public service
  • Opening night execution plan distributed and walked through with all staff
