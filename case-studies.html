Labor Cost Engagement #01

Hotel F&B Department — Labor Reduced from 38% to 29%

A full-service hotel in Miami's urban core was running F&B labor at 38% of revenue — 9 points above industry benchmark. The operation had no scheduling model, no daily labor reporting, and redundant supervisory layers accumulated over three GM transitions.

Leander stepped in as fractional director of operations for 90 days, rebuilt the scheduling model from scratch, eliminated redundant roles, and installed a daily labor tracking dashboard used by all department heads. Labor cost stabilized at 29% by day 60 and held through the engagement close.

Pre-Opening Engagement #02

Miami Beach Restaurant — Opened On Schedule, Under Budget

An independent operator came in with a signed lease, a concept, and no operations team. Eight weeks before the target opening date, the kitchen was unfinished, the hiring pipeline was empty, and no training materials existed. The operator needed a build partner, not just a consultant.

Leander built the hiring funnel, wrote all FOH and BOH SOPs, led two weeks of service simulations, managed the opening-week schedule, and coordinated with the construction team on final walk-throughs. The restaurant opened on its target date with no critical staff gaps and hit first-month revenue projections within 4%.

Operations Recovery Engagement #03

Boutique Hotel F&B — Turned Around in 60 Days

After losing its F&B director, a boutique Miami property saw guest satisfaction scores fall to 3.2 stars and revenue per cover drop 18% in two consecutive quarters. The remaining team was demoralized, service standards had collapsed, and the banquet pipeline had dried up.

Leander joined as interim GM, stabilized daily service within the first week, re-trained the team on standards, rebuilt the banquet sales process, and re-established communication rhythms between front-of-house and kitchen. Within 60 days, guest satisfaction scores recovered to 4.4 and RevPAC returned to prior-director levels.
