What a Fractional GM Actually Does for Your Restaurant

By Leander Mena  ·  Miami, Florida

← Back to Insights

A fractional General Manager is not a consultant who delivers a binder and disappears. A fractional GM is embedded in your operation — present for service, accountable to your P&L, and responsible for real outcomes. The “fractional” part refers to the time commitment and cost structure, not the level of ownership or accountability.

What Changes When a Fractional GM Steps In

Most restaurant owners who bring in fractional leadership are dealing with one of three problems: a key operator just left and the ship needs steadying, a new concept is opening and there is no one to run the build, or the operation is bleeding money and no one internal can diagnose why. In every case, the fractional GM’s first job is to establish clarity — who is doing what, to what standard, and with what accountability.

The First 30 Days

The opening phase of a fractional engagement is diagnostic. I am looking at labor reports, food cost variance, scheduling logic, training documentation, vendor contracts, and guest feedback. I am also on the floor — watching how service runs, how managers communicate, and where the friction points are. By day 30, I have a clear picture of the highest-leverage changes the operation needs.

Labor Is Almost Always the First Lever

In my experience, most independent restaurants carry 3–6% unnecessary labor cost — not because they have too many people, but because scheduling is reactive rather than engineered. Building a forecast-driven schedule, cross-training key positions, and creating clear side-work accountability typically recovers margin without cutting headcount or damaging service quality.

What I Am Not

A fractional GM is not a mystery shopper, not a turnaround consultant writing a 40-page report, and not a trainer who runs a one-day workshop. I am a working operator who takes accountability for your business outcomes for the duration of the engagement. The deliverable is not a document — it is a better-running operation.

Is a Fractional GM Right for Your Restaurant?

If you are an independent operator or small group in Miami who needs experienced leadership without the cost or commitment of a full-time executive hire, a fractional engagement is worth exploring. The best-fit situations are pre-opening builds, post-departure stabilization, and operations recovery where the root cause is unclear. If any of those sound familiar, let’s talk.
