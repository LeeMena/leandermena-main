What Pre-Opening Leadership Actually Looks Like

Most restaurant openings fail not because of bad food or wrong location — they fail because the operational infrastructure was not built before the doors opened. No SOPs, undertrained staff, vendor relationships finalized at the last minute, and a GM figuring it out in real time with paying guests watching.

Leander Mena has directed pre-opening operations at properties including Maska Indian Kitchen + Bar (Michelin-concept) and SLS Brickell. Every engagement follows the same principle: build the systems before you need them.

Common Questions

Pre-Opening FAQ

When should I bring in a pre-opening consultant?

Ideally 90–120 days before your target opening date. This allows time for proper staffing, SOP development, vendor onboarding, and training before opening night. Bringing Leander in earlier is always better — rushing the pre-opening phase is the most common and most costly mistake.

Does this work for hotel F&B pre-openings, not just restaurants?

Yes. Leander Mena has directed pre-opening banquet and F&B operations at SLS Brickell and other Miami hotel properties, covering restaurant, bar, banquet, and in-room dining buildout. The approach scales from a 40-seat restaurant to a 400-cover hotel ballroom.

What happens after the opening?

Engagements typically transition to a fractional leadership model (2–4 days per week) for the first 60–90 days post-open to stabilize operations and address real-world issues as they emerge. The goal is always full handover to the permanent team.
